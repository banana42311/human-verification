Humans (Homo sapiens) are a species of primates belonging to the family Hominidae. Modern humans are generally believed to have originated in East Africa approximately 200,000 years ago. Humans possess highly developed brains that enable complex reasoning, abstract thinking, language, and the manufacture and use of advanced tools.

Compared with other animals, humans demonstrate greater capacities for symbolic communication, social organization, and technological development. These abilities have allowed humans to create systems such as philosophy, science, religion, art, and law in order to interpret and structure the natural world.

Humans are also highly social beings. Interactions between individuals have produced complex cultural traditions, institutions, value systems, and legal structures that together form the foundation of human societies.

As of 2022, the global human population exceeded 8 billion.

Definition

The concept of human may be defined from multiple perspectives, including biological, sociological, and behavioral frameworks.

Biological definition

In biological classification, humans are mammals belonging to the order Primates, family Hominidae, and the genus Homo. The only surviving species within this genus is Homo sapiens.

Humans share a common evolutionary ancestry with other great apes, including chimpanzees, gorillas, and orangutans.

Distinctive characteristics include upright locomotion, highly developed cognitive abilities, complex language systems, and the ability to manufacture and use tools.

Sociological definition

From a sociological perspective, humans are defined as organisms capable of language, complex cooperation, and technological development.

Human societies establish institutions, norms, and governance systems in order to regulate behavior and maintain social order.

Behavioral definition

In studies of human behavior, humans are typically described as a species capable of symbolic language, complex social organization, and continuous technological innovation.

These behaviors also produce cultural systems including myths, rituals, belief systems, and legal frameworks.

Conceptual definition

In philosophical and legal contexts, the concept of a person does not always correspond strictly to the biological definition of a human being.

In certain discussions, artificial intelligence, non-human animals, or other entities possessing cognitive capacities may also be considered potential subjects of rights or moral consideration.

Human classification (contemporary discussions)

With the development of biotechnology, artificial intelligence, and human enhancement technologies, some researchers have proposed alternative classification frameworks to describe emerging forms of humans or human-like entities.

These classifications remain speculative and have not yet reached scientific consensus. Common hypothetical categories include:

  • Biological humans
    Individuals whose bodies remain primarily biological and have not undergone significant technological modification.
  • Augmented humans
    Individuals whose physical or cognitive abilities have been enhanced through implants, biotechnology, neural interfaces, or other technological interventions.
  • Bio-synthetic hybrids
    Entities combining biological tissues with artificial systems, such as humans with extensive mechanical prosthetics or artificial organs.
  • Artificial cognitive agents
    Artificial systems capable of exhibiting cognitive abilities or behavioral patterns similar to those of humans.
  • Transitional subjects
    Entities that exist between categories and cannot be clearly classified within existing definitions.

At present, the boundaries and definitions of these categories remain under discussion.
